  • Principal Investigator
    Zhaohui Gu, PhD
    • 2020-Present: Assistant Professor, Beckman Research Institute, City of Hope
    • 2015-2020: Postdoctoral Research Associate, St Jude Children's Research Hospital
    • 2008-2014: Ph.D Student, Shanghai Jiao Tong University

    Dr. Zhaohui Gu received his bachelor's degree in biomedical engineering from Xi'an Jiao Tong University in 2008, then he joined Dr. Zhu Chen's laboratory for his Ph.D. training at Shanghai Institute of Hematology, Shanghai Jiao Tong University. After receiving a Ph.D. degree in bioinformatics, he joined Dr. Charles Mullighan's group at St. Jude Children's Research Hospital as a postdoctoral fellow in 2015. His research has led to 13 first/co-first authored publications on Nature, Nature Genetics, Nature Communications, Bioinformatics, PNAS, etc. He has been supported by the ASH Scholar Award, LLS Special Fellow Award, and NIH/NCI K99/R00 Award. From June 29th, 2020, Dr. Gu will set up his own lab at City of Hope National Medical Center as an assistant professor.

  • Trainee
    Zunsong Hu, PhD
    • 2020-Present: Postdoctoral Fellow, Beckman Research Institute, City of Hope
    • 2017-2020: Postdoctoral Research Associate, University of Tennessee Health Science Center
    • 2012-2017: Ph.D. Student, Peking Union Medical College

    Dr. Zunsong Hu graduated in Preventive Medicine in 2012 from Huazhong University of Science and Technology. Then he did the Ph.D. training in Fuwai Hospital, Peking Union Medical College and received his Ph.D. degree in epidemiology and health statistics, in 2017. His major scientific interests are in integrated multi-omics research of complex diseases and risk factors.

  • Zhilian Jia, PhD
    • 2020-Present: Postdoctoral Fellow, Beckman Research Institute, City of Hope
    • 2017-2020: Assistant Professor, Shanghai Jiao Tong University
    • 2015-2017: Postdoctoral Fellow, Shanghai Jiao Tong University
    • 2009-2015: Ph.D. Student, Molecular Biology, Shanghai Jiao Tong University

    Dr. Zhilian Jia received her bachelor's degree in Nursing from Peking University in 2009, then she joined Dr. Qiang Wu's laboratory for her Ph.D. training at Shanghai Center for Systems Biomedicine, Shanghai Jiao Tong University. After receiving a Ph.D. degree in Molecular Biology in 2015, she continued her postdoctoral training at Dr. Qiang Wu's laboratory. She has been working as an assistant professor in Shanghai Jiao Tong University since 2017. Her research will be focusing on functional sutdy of driver genetic lesions in novel leukemia subtypes.
